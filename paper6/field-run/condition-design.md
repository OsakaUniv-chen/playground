# Paper 6 — Condition Design (working)

> The experimental comparison. Field-run cost is high → minimal design:
> **2 conditions differing in exactly one variable — attention timing.**

## The two conditions
| Condition | Proactive acknowledgement | Timing | Role |
|---|---|---|---|
| **C1 — Reactive (baseline)** | yes — acknowledges waiting visitors | **reactive**: responds only *after* a conflict surfaces (someone interrupts / repeats) | remedial control |
| **C2 — Full (anticipatory)** | yes — same management | **anticipatory**: PSSP predicts who is about to speak/engage; acknowledges *before* conflict | the integrated system |

Both run the **same proactive policy and the same low-level motion**; they differ **only** in whether acknowledgement timing is PSSP-anticipatory or reactive.

## What C2 − C1 isolates
The **net value of anticipation** (timing), with management held constant — the exact quantity Paper 5 could not measure in a no-consequence task, now tested where attention is consequential.

## Core causal mechanism (why C2 should win)
- **C2 anticipatory = prevention (before):** PSSP foresees the next engager → acknowledge before the conflict happens → conflict prevented.
- **C1 reactive = remediation (after):** can only respond once a conflict has surfaced → already late.

On the primary metric (conflict-event rate), **prevention beats remediation** — a clean, testable causal story, and the mechanistic claim P6 contributes.

## Why "proactive" is not a separate condition
The value of proactive management itself is already established by **P1–P2** (Field B). Both P6 conditions keep proactive management **ON**; P6's microscope is on the **anticipatory** half — Paper 5's open question. The integrated system's overall value is carried by the field deployment itself + the P1–P2 narrative.

## How the primary metrics separate the conditions
- **Conflict-event rate** (interruptions / overlaps / repeats / line-cutting): C2 < C1 if prevention works.
- **Acknowledgement timeliness** (arrival → first-ack latency): C2 shorter, and crucially *before* conflict onset.
- **Secondary** (P5 stack + motion logs: κ, switch rate, smoothness): control the P5 motion-dynamics confound — confirm C1/C2 move *similarly* so any effect is timing, not dynamics.
